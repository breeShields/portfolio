Wall Mural

Student Work

Brief

To create branding and collateral work for a fictitious pizza restaurant chain in Australia. They required an illustration-focused brand that could be easily applied to various franchises opening throughout the country. This included: a name for the restaurant, a custom, hand illustrated logo comprised of the restaurant name as well as some illustrated imagery, and an illustrative composition for a 3 x 6 metre mural on a wall located inside the restaurant.


In order to advertise the restaurant, the client also required a short Instagram ad and a web banner in the same style as the rest of the illustrated elements.

My Contribution

I followed a simple approach when creating the logo. I used several different pens and writing styles until I came up with something that was fitting of a pizza restaurant. After digitising the typeface, I felt that it needed another element, so I began playing with shapes. The shape I came up with signifies pizza sauce, while the choice of the colour red was used to create a spontaneous feeling in the mind of the consumer. The text is white to create a high contrast and to draw the eye of the viewer.


As for the added elements, I decided to use simple colours mainly comprising of those appearing on the ingredients of a pizza: reds, yellows, and greens. I feel that this colour combination is appealing for a restaurant as it generates feelings of ‘fresh’ and ‘food’. This also makes sense considering the added elements are indeed illustrations of pizza ingredients.


The web banner and Instagram ad both utilise the logo, typeface, and the other illustrated elements. The plan was again to make them as simple as possible, while creating feelings of hunger and interest.
